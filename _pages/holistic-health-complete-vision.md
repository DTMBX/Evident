    <h2>The Regions: Where This Matters Most</h2>

    <h3>Appalachia (WV, KY, PA, OH)</h3>
    <p>
      <strong>Crisis:</strong> Highest opioid + antidepressant rates nationally.
      <strong>Cause:</strong> Coal industry collapse, economic despair, limited pain management alternatives.
      <strong>Solution:</strong> Community gardens, opioid reduction clinics, cold-hardy plant education.
    </p>

    <h3>Deep South (LA, MS, AL, AR)</h3>
    <p>
      <strong>Crisis:</strong> Highest poverty + food desert rates; high antacid + antidepressant use.
      <strong>Cause:</strong> Limited healthcare access, processed food dependency, lack of affordable fresh food.
      <strong>Solution:</strong> Warm zones (8‚Äì9) enable year-round gardening. Family + community garden network.
    </p>

    <h3>Sunbelt (FL, AZ, NV)</h3>
    <p>
      <strong>Crisis:</strong> Aging populations with polypharmacy (5+ medications); highest antacid + pain med use.
      <strong>Cause:</strong> Isolation, sedentary aging, preventive overuse by clinicians.
      <strong>Solution:</strong> Community gardening reduces isolation; year-round growing maintains engagement.
    </p>

    <h3>Upper Midwest (OH, IN, IL, MI, WI)</h3>
    <p>
      <strong>Crisis:</strong> High depression + antacid rates; seasonal affective disorder; post-industrial despair.
      <strong>Cause:</strong> Winter darkness, economic decline, sedentary modern work.
      <strong>Solution:</strong> Winter gardening (greenhouses, root cellaring); indoor herb growing; community engagement.
    </p>
