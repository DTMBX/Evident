❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about BarberX Legal Tech

Is my data sent to the cloud? ▼

No. All AI processing happens 100% locally on your machine. Your BWC videos, transcripts, and case data never leave your computer. We don't have access to your evidence files.

What are the subscription costs? ▼

Free Tier: 5 analyses per month, 500MB max file size

Professional ($99/month): 100 analyses/month, 2GB files, API access, batch processing

Enterprise (custom): Unlimited analyses, white-label, dedicated support

How accurate is the transcription? ▼

We use OpenAI's Whisper model which achieves 99%+ accuracy on clear audio. Accuracy depends on:

  • Audio quality (better microphones = better results)
  • Background noise levels
  • Speaker accents and clarity

BWC footage typically achieves 95-98% accuracy.

Is this admissible in court? ▼

Our platform generates court-defensible evidence packages with:

  • SHA-256 chain of custody verification
  • Audit logs of all processing steps
  • Original file preservation
  • Timestamped metadata

Note: Always consult with your legal team. We provide tools for evidence analysis, but attorneys determine admissibility.

What video formats are supported? ▼

We support all common video formats:

  • MP4 (most common for BWC)
  • MOV (Apple devices)
  • AVI, MKV, WebM
  • Any format supported by FFmpeg
How long does analysis take? ▼

Processing time depends on video length and hardware:

  • 10 minute video: 3-5 minutes
  • 1 hour video: 15-20 minutes
  • 8 hour shift: 2-3 hours

GPU acceleration can reduce these times by 50-70%.

Can I process multiple videos at once? ▼

Yes! Our Batch Processor (Professional tier) allows simultaneous processing of up to 20 videos. Perfect for multi-officer incidents or shift-long BWC footage.

Do I need a GPU? ▼

No, but it helps. All AI models work on CPU-only systems. A GPU (NVIDIA with CUDA) will speed up processing by 2-5x, especially for long videos.

Can I export to Word or PDF? ▼

Yes. All analysis reports can be exported to:

  • PDF (professional court exhibits)
  • DOCX (Microsoft Word)
  • TXT (plain text)
  • JSON (structured data for developers)
  • MD (Markdown)
How do you handle chain of custody? ▼

Every file uploaded is immediately hashed with SHA-256. This cryptographic fingerprint proves the file hasn't been altered. All processing steps are logged with timestamps, creating a verifiable audit trail.
