## My Mission

I'm sharing my documented experiences to help others recognize bad actors and systemic
dysfunction. Not to build a platform. Not to corner a market. Just to shine light on
problems honestly and productively.

## Core Philosophy

Documentation should expose truth, not obscure it. Every case shared must serve honesty,
clarity, and helping good people make better decisions ‚Äî never revenge, harassment, or manipulation.

---

## Foundational Principles

### Why Document?

All documentation serves to:

- **Preserve truth** ‚Äî Capture what actually happened with evidence
- **Expose patterns** ‚Äî Bad actors leave recognizable trails
- **Protect others** ‚Äî Share lessons so good people avoid similar traps
- **Maintain integrity** ‚Äî Focus on facts, not spin or narrative

### Documentation Standards

My approach emphasizes:

- **Context preservation** ‚Äî Every claim needs supporting detail
- **Minimal speculation** ‚Äî Stick to what can be verified
- **Pattern focus** ‚Äî Individual incidents reveal systemic issues
- **Protective purpose** ‚Äî Document to inform, not to attack

## How We Get There

### Principles That Guide Our Development

**1. Restraint Over Expansion**
We will systematically refuse temptations to add features that:

- Track individuals or patterns
- Enable mass monitoring
- Facilitate harassment or doxxing
- Substitute for professional oversight
- Promise to "solve" complex social problems

### 2. Citizen-Centered, Not Authority-Dependent

- We serve people, not institutions
- We empower documentation, not enforcement
- We respect constitutional limits, not claim special authority
- We support transparency, not secrecy

### 3. Transparency as Foundation

- Every decision documented and public
- Governance frameworks published before features
- Community input integrated before deployment
- Limitations and risks clearly stated

### 4. Legal Sobriety Always

- We do not provide legal advice or authority
- We respect jurisdictional differences
- We emphasize user responsibility
- We connect people with qualified counsel

### 5. Continuous Accountability

- Regular review of whether we're meeting our mission
- Community feedback mechanisms active
- Willingness to acknowledge mistakes
- Continuous improvement cycles

## Risks We Acknowledge

### What Could Go Wrong‚ÄîAnd How We're Preparing

#### Risk: Tool Misuse for Harassment or Doxxing

- Mitigation: Built-in consent management, data minimization, no personal data export
- Response: Community moderation, user education, clear consequences
- Design: Technical barriers to weaponizing documentation

### Risk: Substitution for Professional Oversight

- Mitigation: Documentation explicitly supports, not replaces, legal processes
- Response: Training emphasizes due process, citizen documentation limitations
- Design: Tools integrate with, not around, lawful authorities

### Risk: Privacy Erosion Over Time

- Mitigation: No cloud dependency, encrypted local storage, user control
- Response: Regular audits, community oversight, transparency reports
- Design: Architectural choices prevent surveillance creep

### Risk: Regulatory Capture or Mission Drift

- Mitigation: Clear governance, published standards, community accountability
- Response: Structured review processes, transparent decision-making
- Design: Decentralized architecture prevents centralized control

### Risk: Adoption by Bad Actors

- Mitigation: User education, consent requirements, ethical framework
- Response: Community mechanisms to identify and address misuse
- Design: Tools designed for accountability, not coercion
