<section class="section-block">
  <div class="container">
    <h2>Key Findings</h2>

    <div class="stat-card">
      <h3>National Context</h3>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>70% of Americans</strong> take at least one prescription drug (CDC 2023)</li>
        <li><strong>46%</strong> take at least two; <strong>24%</strong> take five or more</li>
        <li><strong>Most common:</strong> antidepressants, opioids, antacids, statins, antidiabetics</li>
        <li><strong>Cost burden:</strong> Americans spend $400+ billion annually on prescription drugs</li>
      </ul>
    </div>

    <div class="stat-card">
      <h3>Overmedication Risks</h3>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Polypharmacy:</strong> 5+ medications increase adverse event risk exponentially</li>
        <li><strong>Drug-drug interactions:</strong> Estimated 100,000+ preventable deaths yearly</li>
        <li><strong>Deprescribing:</strong> Growing physician movement to safely reduce unnecessary medications</li>
        <li><strong>Preventable disease:</strong> 80% of chronic disease is lifestyle-related (food, movement, stress)</li>
      </ul>
    </div>

  </div>
</section>

    <h2>Top Medication Classes & Plant-Based Food Support</h2>

    <div class="grid-2">

      <div class="stat-card">
        <h3>Antidepressants (SSRIs)</h3>
        <p class="muted">~30 million Americans; highest rates in upper Midwest, West Virginia</p>
        <p><strong>Lifestyle root causes:</strong> Chronic stress, sleep deprivation, sedentary behavior, refined diet</p>
        <p><strong>Food-first plants:</strong></p>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>Tulsi (Holy Basil)</strong> – adaptogen; gentle mood & nervous system support</li>
          <li><strong>Peppermint</strong> – calming tea</li>
          <li><strong>Omega-3 plants:</strong> Flax, chia, walnuts (mood & inflammation)</li>
          <li><strong>B-complex foods:</strong> Leafy greens, legumes, whole grains</li>
          <li><strong>Movement + sleep:</strong> Non-negotiable foundational changes</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

      <div class="stat-card">
        <h3>Opioids</h3>
        <p class="muted">~10 million Americans; crisis in Appalachia (WV, KY, OH, PA)</p>
        <p><strong>Root causes:</strong> Chronic pain from injury, poor posture, inflammation; inadequate physical therapy</p>
        <p><strong>Plant-based pain support:</strong></p>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>Turmeric + black pepper + fat</strong> – potent anti-inflammatory</li>
          <li><strong>Ginger</strong> – circulation, inflammation</li>
          <li><strong>Rosemary</strong> – topical oil for massage; circulation</li>
          <li><strong>Movement:</strong> Physical therapy, stretching, walking</li>
          <li><strong>Sleep:</strong> Quality sleep amplifies pain tolerance</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

      <div class="stat-card">
        <h3>Antacids (PPIs)</h3>
        <p class="muted">~20 million Americans; highest in South and Upper Midwest</p>
        <p><strong>Root causes:</strong> Refined diet, stress, irregular eating, processed foods</p>
        <p><strong>Food-first approach:</strong></p>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>Ginger tea</strong> – digestive support before meals</li>
          <li><strong>Peppermint</strong> – calming to GI tract</li>
          <li><strong>Fermented foods:</strong> Sauerkraut, kimchi (beneficial flora)</li>
          <li><strong>Bone broth</strong> – healing to gut lining</li>
          <li><strong>Whole foods only;</strong> eliminate processed + fried</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

      <div class="stat-card">
        <h3>Statins</h3>
        <p class="muted">~30 million Americans; preventive overuse in older populations</p>
        <p><strong>Root causes:</strong> Inflammatory diet, sedentary, high stress</p>
        <p><strong>Food-first support:</strong></p>
        <ul>
          <li><strong>Oily fish, nuts, seeds</strong> – omega-3 fatty acids</li>
          <li><strong>Garlic, onions</strong> – circulation; mild cholesterol support</li>
          <li><strong>Leafy greens</strong> – magnesium, folate</li>
          <li><strong>Regular movement</strong> – most powerful lever</li>
          <li><strong>Stress management</strong> – inflammation driver</li>
        </ul>
      </div>

    <h2>Overmedication by Region</h2>

    <h3>Appalachia (WV, KY, OH, PA)</h3>
    <p>
      <strong>Challenge:</strong> Highest opioid + antidepressant rates. Legacy of coal industry injuries,
      economic decline, and limited access to physical therapy.
    </p>
    <p>
      <strong>Stewardship response:</strong> Cold-hardy gardens (zones 5–7) + community-building.
      Garlic, onions, cold-tolerant greens, peppermint, chamomile grow easily in Appalachian climates.
      Home food production reduces processed diet; community gardens reduce isolation.
    </p>

    <h3>Deep South (WV, KY, LA, AL, MS)</h3>
    <p>
      <strong>Challenge:</strong> High rates of antacids, opioids, antidepressants. High poverty, food deserts,
      limited preventive care access.
    </p>
    <p>
      <strong>Stewardship response:</strong> Warm zones (7–9) allow year-round growing. Turmeric, ginger,
      okra, sweet potatoes, leafy greens thrive in containers or small yards.
      Multi-generational family gardening reclaims cultural food traditions and reduces medication dependency.
    </p>

    <h3>Upper Midwest (OH, IN, IL, MI)</h3>
    <p>
      <strong>Challenge:</strong> High depression rates (winter, industrial decline), antacid use (processed diet),
      seasonal affective patterns.
    </p>
    <p>
      <strong>Stewardship response:</strong> Zones 5–6 favor cold-hardy herbs + preserved foods.
      Peppermint, chamomile, garlic, onions, preserved greens. Root cellaring + fermentation culture
      extends harvest. Greenhouses enable winter tulsi + ginger in containers.
    </p>

    <h3>Florida & Sunbelt (FL, AZ, TX)</h3>
    <p>
      <strong>Challenge:</strong> Aging populations, high polypharmacy, antacids, statins, pain medications.
    </p>
    <p>
      <strong>Stewardship response:</strong> Year-round growing (zones 8–11). Turmeric, ginger, okra, peppers,
      sweet potatoes, leafy greens available 11 months/year. Community gardening reduces isolation;
      multigenerational nutrition improves medication tolerance.
    </p>

  </div>
</section>
