    <h2>The Story</h2>
    <p>
      Devon Tyler grew up in two households. Order and responsibility were paired with distance and
      unanswered questions. That experience produced a hunger for meaning without bitterness toward the
      structures that provided stability. It also created an obligation to handle facts carefully and to
      welcome oversight.
    </p>
    <p>
      The pandemic season tested that obligation. Institutions strained under uncertainty, families felt
      pressure, and public trust was shaken. Devon asked hard questions about process and truth while
      respecting lawful authority. The resulting tension clarified a calling: build a home for honesty,
      repentance, and verifiable recordkeeping rather than grievance.
    </p>

    <h2 style="margin-top: 3rem;">The Awakening</h2>
    <p>
      Scripture, history, and civic education offered direction. They reinforced that faith forms conscience
      and character; the Constitution structures public life; and transparency protects both. Faith Frontier
      therefore pairs spiritual reflection with constitutional literacy so neighbors know how to engage courts,
      statutes, and agencies without fear.
    </p>
    <p>
      The work is not rebellion. It is stewardship: open records, clear timelines, and accurate citations that
      anyone can verify.
    </p>

    <h2 style="margin-top: 3rem;">The Declaration</h2>
    <p>
      Faith Frontier exists to rebuild trust at human scale. It is a commitment to lawful transparency, to
      humility about personal story, and to the belief that repentance and accountability belong together. The
      manifesto invites citizens to engage local governance, zoning boards, courts, and public agencies with
      calm, documented care.
    </p>

    <div style="margin: 2.5rem 0; padding: 1.75rem; border-radius: 0.75rem; border: 1px solid var(--accent-emerald); background: rgba(15, 118, 110, 0.12); box-shadow: 0 6px 18px rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.25);">
      <p style="margin: 0; font-size: 1.1rem; line-height: 1.8; font-style: italic; color: var(--color-highlight);">
        <strong>Guiding Charge:</strong> Tell the truth. Respect due process. Publish evidence. Invite
        correction. Faith informs conscience; law orders civic life.
      </p>
    </div>

    <div style="padding: 2rem; background: rgba(176, 137, 104, 0.1); border-radius: 0.75rem; margin: 2rem 0; border: 1px solid var(--accent-brass);">
      <p style="margin: 0; font-size: 1.1rem; line-height: 1.8;">
        <strong>We honor the past with gratitude, not grievance.</strong><br>
        <strong>We name the present with evidence, not alarm.</strong><br>
        <strong>We build the future with lawful transparency and local accountability.</strong>
      </p>
    </div>

    <p style="font-size: 1.2rem; font-weight: 600; color: var(--accent-emerald); text-align: center; margin: 2rem 0;">
      Faith Frontier is the work of being <em>in the world, but not of it</em> — and always within the courts
      and statutes that govern us.
    </p>

    <h2 style="margin-top: 3rem;">The Principles</h2>

    <div style="display: grid; grid-template-columns: repeat(auto-fit, minmax(250px, 1fr)); gap: 1.5rem; margin: 2rem 0;">

      <article style="padding: 1.75rem; background: rgba(15, 23, 42, 0.4); border-radius: 0.5rem; border-top: 3px solid var(--accent-emerald);">
        <h3 style="margin-top: 0; color: var(--accent-emerald);">Truthfulness & Evidence</h3>
        <p style="margin-bottom: 0;">
          Publish documents, dates, and citations that others can verify. Separate belief statements from legal
          claims so readers know what is faith and what is record.
        </p>
      </article>

      <article style="padding: 1.75rem; background: rgba(15, 23, 42, 0.4); border-radius: 0.5rem; border-top: 3px solid var(--accent-emerald);">
        <h3 style="margin-top: 0; color: var(--accent-emerald);">Conscience & Law</h3>
        <p style="margin-bottom: 0;">
          Faith forms conscience, but courts, statutes, and due process govern civic action. We work within that
          framework and welcome review.
        </p>
      </article>

      <article style="padding: 1.75rem; background: rgba(15, 23, 42, 0.4); border-radius: 0.5rem; border-top: 3px solid var(--accent-emerald);">
        <h3 style="margin-top: 0; color: var(--accent-emerald);">Local Accountability</h3>
        <p style="margin-bottom: 0;">
          Steward land, housing, and partnerships with clear contracts, proper licensing, and respect for zoning boards and
          worker protections.
        </p>
      </article>

      <article style="padding: 1.75rem; background: rgba(15, 23, 42, 0.4); border-radius: 0.5rem; border-top: 3px solid var(--accent-brass);">
        <h3 style="margin-top: 0; color: var(--accent-brass);">Transparency Over Suspicion</h3>
        <p style="margin-bottom: 0;">
          Avoid speculation and conspiracy thinking. If something is alleged, show the document. If something is unknown,
          say so plainly and invite clarification.
        </p>
      </article>

      <article style="padding: 1.75rem; background: rgba(15, 23, 42, 0.4); border-radius: 0.5rem; border-top: 3px solid var(--accent-brass);">
        <h3 style="margin-top: 0; color: var(--accent-brass);">Humility Over Certainty</h3>
        <p style="margin-bottom: 0;">
          Hold narratives lightly, correct errors quickly, and keep hospitality at the center. Moral witness does not replace
          civil authority.
        </p>
      </article>

      <article style="padding: 1.75rem; background: rgba(15, 23, 42, 0.4); border-radius: 0.5rem; border-top: 3px solid var(--accent-brass);">
        <h3 style="margin-top: 0; color: var(--accent-brass);">Service Over Spectacle</h3>
        <p style="margin-bottom: 0;">
          Focus on housing stability, lawful work, recovery pathways, and public education — not performative outrage or
          culture-war rhetoric.
        </p>
      </article>

    </div>

    <h2 style="margin-top: 3rem;">The Commitment</h2>
    <p>
      Faith Frontier commits to:
    </p>
    <ul style="line-height: 1.9;">
      <li><strong>Record integrity</strong> — No deliberate misrepresentation of fact, record, or lived experience; errata are logged when found.</li>
      <li><strong>Due-process literacy</strong> — Guides that help neighbors use courts, administrative bodies, and public-record systems appropriately.</li>
      <li><strong>Open correction</strong> — A public invitation for agencies, journalists, and citizens to review documents and flag mistakes.</li>
      <li><strong>Local stewardship</strong> — Agreements, trusts, and LLCs that respect tax law, zoning, licensing, and corporate separateness.</li>
      <li><strong>Faithful posture</strong> — Humility, repentance, and service as the tone for all advocacy.</li>
    </ul>

    <h2 style="margin-top: 3rem;">The Invitation</h2>
    <p>
      This manifesto is for anyone who wants to pair faith-informed conscience with lawful civic action. It welcomes public
      servants, neighbors, journalists, attorneys, and congregations to review the record, ask questions, and help refine the
      work. Nothing here seeks secret knowledge or shadow authority — only transparent stewardship that others can inspect.
    </p>
    <p>
      If you share that aim, this frontier is yours too.
    </p>

  </article>
