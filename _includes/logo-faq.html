Frequently Asked Questions

What does the Faith Frontier logo represent?

It is a Christ-centered ecclesiastical seal that expresses our faith commitments and the order of Christian life: the Father’s authority, the Son’s redemption, and the Spirit’s renewing work—communicated through restrained form and color.

Is Faith Frontier a church or a nonprofit?

Faith Frontier is a faith-rooted stewardship project with theological convictions. Legal status depends on jurisdiction and formal filings, and we do not claim any civil classification unless and until it is formally established.

Why does Faith Frontier use “temple” language?

In Christian theology, Scripture teaches that the people of God are the temple—that God dwells not in buildings alone, but within His people (1 Corinthians 3:16–17).

When Faith Frontier uses “temple” language, it is expressing a biblical understanding of spiritual identity, not making a legal, regulatory, or tax-related claim. Civil status is determined separately by applicable law.

Why use a seal instead of a modern logo?

A seal communicates continuity, accountability, and seriousness of purpose. It signals a commitment to stewardship and public trust rather than trend-driven branding.

Can the logo be used publicly?

Yes, when referencing Faith Frontier in good faith and using official assets. The logo must not be altered, recolored outside the approved palette, or used in misleading contexts.
